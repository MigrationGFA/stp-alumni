The “Stanford Seed Transformation Program (STP)” is a high-impact initiative by the Stanford Graduate School of Business (GSB) designed to help established entrepreneurs in emerging economies scale their businesses. 
While the program is led by Stanford faculty, Stanford alumni play a pivotal role as the primary "engine" of the project, serving as volunteer business coaches and consultants.
 1. The Core Program: Seed Transformation Program (STP)
This is a 10-month leadership and business growth journey for CEOs and founders of companies in Africa, South Asia, and Indonesia.
Target Audience: Established for-profit businesses with annual revenues between $300,000 and $15 million.
The "Transformation Plan": The centerpiece of the program is the creation of a detailed, actionable plan to scale the company.
Curriculum: Led by Stanford GSB faculty, it covers strategy, finance, marketing, and operations, but is tailored to the challenges of emerging markets.
 2. The Role of Stanford Alumni
Stanford alumni are deeply integrated into the program in two distinct ways:
Volunteer Coaches & Consultants
The program relies on seasoned Stanford GSB alumni and other senior executives who volunteer their time (often staying in-country for months) to work directly with participating CEOs. 
Coaching: Alumni help founders shift from "working *in* the business" to "working *on* the business."
Consulting: Specialized alumni teams often provide pro bono consulting on specific projects, such as supply chain optimization or digital transformation.
 Alumni Consulting Team (ACT) Projects
A specific initiative called Stanford Seed II was conducted by the Stanford GSB Alumni Consulting Team (ACT). This project focused on:
Improving the investor readiness of companies that have graduated from the Seed program.
Bridging the gap between Seed entrepreneurs and global investors (including alumni investors).
 3. The Seed Transformation Network (STN)
Once a leader completes the program, they join the Seed Transformation Network, which is the "alumni network" for the program itself. 
Community: Over 1,500 members across 30+ countries.
Collaboration: 42% of network members report doing business with one another.
Continuous Learning: Members have ongoing access to Stanford resources, student interns, and peer-to-peer "Leadership Labs."

 4. Key Impact Metrics (As of 2026)
The project has demonstrated significant measurable success:
Revenue Growth: Participating companies see a 29% median increase in revenue within one year of completion.
Capital Raised: Over $2 billion in collective capital (loans, equity, and grants) has been secured by Seed companies.
Job Creation: An estimated 1.6 million livelihoods have been impacted through direct and indirect job creation.
Net Set 2026/2027 Cohort: Applications typically open in January and close in May.

The goal of the first login is to turn that data into a "networking-ready" profile. To make it feel like a professional, Stanford-level ecosystem:
1. The "STP Identity" (The Glue)
Since this is an alumni site, the "When and Where" of their Stanford experience is the primary filter for trust.
Cohort Year: (e.g., 2022, 2023).

Cohort Name (Optional): Some cohorts have "nicknames" or specific groupings.
2. The Business Profile (“Entity Logic")
In the STP world, the individual is inseparable from their company. You need to capture the entity they represent.
Company Name & Website: Essential for due diligence.
Company Stage/Size (Optional): Use brackets rather than exact numbers to protect privacy (e.g., 10–50 employees, 50–200 employees).
Business Model: B2B, B2C, Marketplace, etc. (This helps with peer-matching).
3. The "Give and Get" (Marketplace & Deal Room Actionable Data)
Bios and profile preview. Copied the "Leadership Labs" model.
"I can help with...": (Dropdown/Tags) e.g., Raising Series A, Scaling to new markets, Supply chain in Lagos, Government relations.
"I'm looking for...": (Dropdown/Tags) e.g., Investment, Technical co-founders, Export partners, Local distributors.
4. Advanced Location Data (optional)
Since STP is global but rooted in specific hubs:
Primary Market: Where they currently operate.
Expansion Interests: Where they want to go. (e.g., "Based in Accra, looking to expand to Nairobi").

Critical Feature: The "Admin Pre-Fill"
Since your onboarding is admin-dependent, you can "delight" the user by having 60% of their profile already finished.
The Logic: When the admin approves the user, they upload a CSV/JSON with the user's STP graduation data.
User Experience: Upon first login, the user sees: "Welcome, [Name]! We've pre-filled your STP 2023 West Africa details. Does this look right?"
One Small Warning: "Skills" vs. "Expertise"
For LinkedIn-inspired sites, "Skills" can often feel a bit "entry-level" (e.g., "Microsoft Excel"). For STP Alumni, I recommend renaming this field to "Areas of Expertise" or "Advisory Strengths" to reflect their status as senior leaders.
